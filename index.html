Hi, I am Zulfiya Usmonova~

zulfiya.usmonova@tum.de

I am an MSc student in AI in Society at the Technical University of Munich and a student research assistant at the Chair for Human-Centered Technologies for Learning. I study how language-model systems behave when information is repeatedly transformed, agents influence one another, and tasks change over time.

My broader goal is to build evaluation methods that reveal failures static benchmarks often miss. My work spans AI evaluation and benchmarking, LLM robustness, human–AI interaction, and multi-agent systems, with a growing focus on dynamic and interactive evaluation for educational and agentic AI.

I enjoy turning research questions into reproducible experiments and working systems—from locally hosted LLM evaluation pipelines to multi-agent simulations and educational games. Outside research, I draw digital illustrations and occasionally disappear into Unity projects 🪐

Research & Publications

Most AI benchmarks ask a model to answer a fixed set of questions once. I am more interested in what happens when a system must act over time: preserve information, respond to other agents, adapt to changing states, and recover from mistakes.

My projects combine controlled experiments, human reference data, locally hosted and open-weight LLMs, and interactive simulations to study these failures in a reproducible way.

Dynamic & Interactive Benchmarking for Reliable AI Systems

Master’s thesis in preparation under Prof. Enkelejda Kasneci. I am designing a modular environment in which tasks evolve in response to an AI system’s actions, allowing direct comparison between static, dynamic, and interactive evaluation.

The benchmark will examine long-horizon planning, memory use, action validity, adaptation, and recovery from mistakes in changing environments.
